The Future of Human-AI Trust Calibration

How insurance-inspired mechanisms can save us from over-reliance on automated systems.

As AI systems become increasingly integrated into our daily decision-making processes, a critical challenge emerges: **Trust Calibration**. We often find ourselves in a binary state of either total skepticism or blind over-reliance. Neither is optimal for safety.

The "TrustGuard" Concept

In my current research, I explore the idea of "interface friction." Imagine an AI system that doesn't just give you an answer, but requires a "digital deductible"—a small moment of forced reflection—before you can accept its output in high-stakes scenarios.

"The goal isn't to make people trust AI more; it's to make them trust it exactly as much as they should."

By borrowing principles from behavioral economics and insurance theory, we can design interfaces that proactively guide user behavior toward verification when the AI's confidence is low.
